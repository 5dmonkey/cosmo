Family Proposal

Incoming First Grade Family Campout

A playful weekend to help kids and families connect at the beginning of the school year.

A warm note from a few incoming first grade families

We’ve been talking about how meaningful it could be for families to have a chance to connect before the school year really gets going — in a way that feels easy, low-pressure, and genuinely enjoyable for both kids and parents.

Our hope is to create a simple weekend in nature where children can begin forming friendships early, and where parents can start building a sense of connection and support too. We imagine something playful, relaxed, and thoughtfully organized — not overly produced, just warm and welcoming.

This is meant to be a starting point, not a fixed plan. We’d love ideas, feedback, and collaboration from other families so the weekend can feel as comfortable, accessible, and community-oriented as possible.

Event Details

Tentative Date
September 18–20, 2026
Potential Location
Lake Cachuma (Dakota Plains campsite) or a nearby Ojai campground
Estimated Size
About 10 families
Approximate Cost
About $175 per family

This estimate is intended simply to cover shared costs — including campsite reservation, a few shared meals, and a handful of activities — while helping create a comfortable, welcoming experience for families.

Weekend Schedule

Friday

Arrival + Connection

3:00–5:00 PM
Families arrive and settle into camp
A gentle arrival window so everyone has time to get set up, orient themselves, and ease into the weekend.
5:00 PM
Opening Gathering + Welcome Game
A playful group activity for both kids and parents to begin getting to know one another.
5:45 PM
Kids Field Games + Parent Mingle
Facilitators lead games for the children nearby while parents have a chance to connect. Strong parent community can make a real difference in helping both families and children feel supported throughout the school year.
6:30 PM
Community Dinner
Shared hamburgers, hot dogs, and simple family-style food.
7:30 PM
Bedtime Story Experience
A cozy storytelling and music moment for the children before bedtime.
8:30 PM onward
Parent Campfire Hangout
Relaxed adult time around the fire while designated babysitters keep an eye on sleeping tents and remain available by phone or walkie-talkie if needed.

Saturday

Play + Rest

Morning
Slow morning + breakfast at camp
Families ease into the day at their own pace.
Late Morning / Early Afternoon
Family Field Day
Playful outdoor games and activities for both kids and adults.
Afternoon
Kids Adventure Session + Parent Rest Time
Facilitators lead children in games and activities while parents have time to relax, recharge, nap, read, or connect.
Evening
Community Gathering
Simple stories, music, performances, or shared activities together.
Dinner
Flexible Family Dinner
Families can cook at camp or bring dinner into the communal area while music plays and children roam and play.
Night
Community S’mores Night
A relaxed, cozy close to the day around the fire.

Sunday

Closing Morning

Morning
Community Breakfast
One final easy shared meal before the weekend winds down.
Late Morning
Closing Play + Pack Up
Children continue playing together while families slowly pack up camp.
Before Departure
Final Goodbye Gathering
A simple closing moment to wrap the weekend and head home feeling more connected.

Camping Support

We know families will have different levels of camping experience and gear. Some may already have extras to share, and some may need a little support. We’ll do our best to help connect families directly where possible so the weekend can feel easier and more accessible.

Co-Host Feedback + Collaboration

We’d love thoughts, ideas, and collaboration as this takes shape. A few helpful questions:

  • What feels exciting?
  • What would help this feel easiest for families?
  • Any ideas for activities or shared moments?
  • Any accessibility or support needs we should consider?
This weekend is intended to feel warm, safe, simple, and easy to say yes to — a thoughtful beginning-of-year gathering rooted in connection, nature, and community care.
